// diagnosis, done properly

Fault-finding is a discipline, not a guess.

When the supervisor suggests a likely cause, the diagnostic laptop is connected first: ECU parameters, live data, and fault history are read before any component is replaced. The result is a faster repair, a lower parts bill, and less machine downtime.

// diagnostics on a stuck-in-gear loader Live fault codes. Live data. Live problem-solving.

Reads the codes. Not the bullshit.

OEM-grade diagnostic software — Cummins INSITE, Caterpillar ET, Volvo PTT, Komatsu, Sandvik, and RCT. Codes are interpreted against live data and machine history; a fault is not cleared without identifying and addressing the underlying cause.

  • Live data stream analysis — not just fault codes
  • Wiring diagram overlay before the multimeter comes out
  • Calibrations, parameter changes, software updates
  • Comms across J1939, J1708, CAN-bus, OBD-II
  • Doesn't clear a code without fixing what's behind it

// the bloke

Clear comms, clean work, signed-off outcomes.

Over a decade across the trade — workshop floors, race paddocks, road-train breakdowns, and more Pilbara decline drives than he can count. He diagnoses first, talks second, and charges honestly. No "she'll be right," no bodged repairs, no surprise invoices.

He treats every machine like it's his own: clean terminations, correct fusing, sensible routing. If he wouldn't put it in his own ute, he won't put it in yours. Won't let a faulty unit leave the workshop — signs off only on work that meets specification.

Equally comfortable on remote and fly-in-fly-out sites. Self-sufficient, organised, used to working in tough conditions with whatever's on hand. Toolbox, laptop, head full of wiring diagrams, and a thermos. Doesn't need a supervisor breathing down his neck to complete the job.
